Daily Itinerary

Your perfect week, planned out

Day 1 Friday, Nov 14

Arrival & Welcome

Arrive at PVR airport and transfer to Almar Resort (~20 min). Check in and enjoy welcome drinks at the beach club. Evening stroll along the Malecón oceanfront promenade, watch sunset from Los Muertos Pier, and grab authentic street tacos.

Airport Transfer Hotel Check-in Malecón Walk Street Food
Day 2 Saturday, Nov 15

Beach Club & Nightlife

Relax at Mantamar Beach Club with cabanas and swim-up bar. Late breakfast at The Top rooftop bar. Explore Zona Romántica streets and Olas Altas Saturday Market. Evening: dinner at La Palapa or Café de Olla, then bar-hop along Ignacio L. Vallarta.

Beach Club Saturday Market La Palapa Dinner Bar Hopping

Day 3 Sunday, Nov 16

Bay Cruise & Adventure

Catamaran or yacht cruise to Yelapa or Las Caletas ($120-$150/person, includes open bar and lunch). Swim, snorkel, and cliff-jump in hidden coves. Alternative: Sayulita & San Pancho tour for surfing vibes. Evening: casual dinner, Piano Bar at Casa Cupula, or drag show at Act2PV.

Culture, Art & Spa

Morning: explore Cuale Island markets, visit Galería Olinalá and art galleries, photo stop at Our Lady of Guadalupe Church. Afternoon spa packages at Almar Resort ($80-$150). Fine dining at Café des Artistes (tasting menu ~$80), then cocktails at El Colibrí speakeasy.

Adventure & Tequila

Snorkeling at Los Arcos Marine Park or zip-lining through Sierra Madre jungle ($80-$110). Afternoon tequila tasting tour at Mundo de Tequila ($50-$75/person). Dinner at Gaby's Restaurant, then dance at Mandala or Strana super-clubs with VIP entry.

Travel Tips

Everything you need to know before you go

📅

Book Early

November is high season in Puerto Vallarta. Book flights and accommodations as soon as possible to secure the best rates and availability. Consider adding Priority boarding for easier travel.

🚕

Transportation

Use official airport taxis or arrange private transfers through the hotel (~$25-30). Zona Romántica is walkable, but taxis and Ubers are affordable for longer distances.

💵

Currency

Mexico uses pesos (MXN). Many places accept USD, but paying in pesos usually gives better rates. Bring cash for tips and markets. ATMs are widely available.

🛂

Documentation

Ensure passports are valid for at least 6 months beyond travel dates. U.S. citizens don't need visas for tourism, but always check current entry requirements before departure.

💧

Health & Safety

Puerto Vallarta is generally safe. Stay aware at night, drink bottled water, use reef-safe sunscreen, and stay hydrated in the tropical climate.

📱

Stay Connected

Check with your carrier about international plans. The resort offers complimentary Wi-Fi. Consider purchasing a local SIM card for better data coverage around the city.
